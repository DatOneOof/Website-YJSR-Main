The Axolotl: An Intriguing Pokémon

Some might find it cute, some might find it weird, but its regenerative abilities are undeniably and scientifically fascinating.

Posted by Maria Eduarda de Mattos Brandão on May 2, 2023

Introduction

The axolotl (Ambystoma mexicanum) must be familiar to many people for being the inspiration to the Pokémon Wooper, or because of its cute appearance, sometimes showing a little smile. This endangered salamander, however, has a remarkable ability to regenerate its limbs, spinal cord, heart, and other organs throughout its lifetime. This means that if an axolotl loses a limb, it can grow a new one from the stump.

Regeneration in Axolotls

The intricate process of regeneration in axolotls involves several steps. First, a structure called a blastema is formed at the site of the injury. The blastema is a mass of undifferentiated cells that will eventually originate the new tissue. Next, the blastema begins to differentiate into the various cell types needed to form the missing structure. This process is guided by a complex network of signals involving various growth factors and other signaling molecules. The cause of this amazing ability is a unique combination of factors such as the large amount of stem cells in the axolotl’s bodies and their cellular dedifferentiation property, which consists of an already differentiated cell being able to return to its undifferentiated state.

Applications of the Ability of Regeneration in Axolotles

Due to this mechanism, axolotls have drawn the attention of researchers. There are several potential applications for axolotl regeneration in medicine, including tissue engineering, wound healing, and regenerative medicine.

One potential use of axolotl regeneration in medicine is in the development of new treatments for spinal cord injuries. Every year, between 250,000 and 500,000 patients suffer a spinal cord injury, which can often result in paralysis. Hence, researchers have carried on many experiments focusing on the axolotl’s spinal cord and found out that the axolotl can regenerate this structure after injury. A 2014 study published at the biomedical research journal Disease Models & Mechanisms compared the spinal cord regeneration on these salamanders and on rats. The results suggested that the mechanisms involved in this process may be similar to both species. By studying the axolotl's regeneration process, researchers may be able to develop new therapies to promote spinal cord regeneration in humans. It can also lead to new applications on limb and tissue injuries regeneration.

Moreover, the axolotl's regeneration ability has also generated interest in the field of regenerative medicine more broadly. Researchers are investigating the use of stem cells and other regenerative techniques to treat a range of conditions, and the axolotl's unique regenerative abilities may provide valuable insights into the mechanisms underlying these processes.

Conclusion

Thus, this exotic creature might be extremely valuable for advances in human medicine. Nonetheless, it is important to acknowledge that the Ambystoma mexicanum is disappearing from nature. Its importance to science strengthens the urgence of the protection of this specie on its habitat and brings up the discussions about the ethics of animal usage for human benefit.
