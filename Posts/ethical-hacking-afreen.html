Ethical Hacking

Posted by Afreen Hossain on August 6, 2023

Introduction

Today, practically everyone engages in some type of internet activity. We have a lot of things saved—passwords, photos, and so on—but where are they all stored? Do we have access to our data alone? Do others not know about it? Today, let's find out. Protecting computer systems from cyberattacks, hacking, unauthorised access, etc. is known as ethical hackers. use cutting-edge technology and a number of additional tools to defend computer systems from such attacks. This discipline covers a wide range of topics, including user awareness training, malware detection and prevention, data encryption, network security, and incident response. We require more ethical hackers now that the globe is moving more towards online work and business because we need professionals to protect our data. Learn more about ethical hackers.

Let's begin by hacking first. If someone tries to gain information from someone but is unsuccessful, and if they are skilled at hacking, they can access the person's mobile device, computer, personal information, etc., and learn information. When someone tries to obtain information about something or someone without having permission, this is referred to as hacking.

Types of hackers

  • Black hat hackers: Black hat hackers, commonly referred to as "crackers" or "dark-side hackers," are those who participate in unauthorised actions and cyberattacks with the intention of harming others. They do it for a variety of reasons, such as wanting to make money or intending to ruin someone's life by publishing intimate information about them. Black hat hackers operate secretly since their activities are illegal, and they risk severe penalties if discovered. Around the world, governments and law enforcement organisations are working hard to locate and capture these hackers so that people, businesses, and essential infrastructures are protected from their damaging activities.
  • White hat hackers:White hat hackers, commonly referred to as "ethical hackers," are those who employ their hacking abilities for legitimate and beneficial goals. In order to enhance security and defend against possible cyber attacks, they strive to detect vulnerabilities and flaws in computer systems, networks, and software. Additionally, they are authorised to break into the computers of the concerned businesses in order to look for vulnerabilities, report them to the businesses, and stop black hat hackers from discovering the holes first.
  • Grey hat hackers:Grey hat hackers are a hybrid of white hat and black hat hackers. They participate in hacking operations without express permission, but they do not just do so with harmful or destructive purposes.Without the owners' consent, grey hat hackers frequently find security holes and vulnerabilities in computer networks, software, and systems. They may take use of these flaws to obtain access without authorization or make the flaws known to the public, frequently with the goal of raising awareness of the security problems and promoting their fix. Grey hat hacking involves activities that might be seen as both beneficial and potentially detrimental, being within a moral and legal grey area.

The steps involved in the penetration testing process, commonly referred to as ethical hacking, may be summed up as follows:

  • Reconnaissance and planning:An ethical hacker acquires data on the target system or network during the reconnaissance and planning stage of ethical hacking. Utilising tools like Nmap and Shodan to find open ports, services, and possible vulnerabilities entails undertaking both passive and active reconnaissance to gather information from publicly accessible sources. The knowledge acquired aids the hacker in planning the remaining penetration test phases, understanding the target environment, and locating prospective entry points. This step lays the framework for the ethical hacker to continue the testing process and concentrate their efforts on areas of vulnerability by adhering to legal and ethical limitations.
  • Scanning:The goal of ethical hacking's scanning phase is to look for open ports, services, and vulnerabilities on the target system or network. This calls for the use of several tools, including Nmap, Nessus, and OpenVAS. The ethical hacker may identify services and do thorough port scanning with Nmap, which helps them comprehend the network structure. The scanning step helps identify possible entry points by giving crucial information about the target's security posture.
  • Gaining access: In the gaining access stage of ethical hacking, vulnerabilities found during scanning are taken advantage of to enter a system without authorization. As well as identifying possible security vulnerabilities and making suggestions for mitigating them, the objective is to establish unauthorised access and sustain persistence within the target system or network. In order to assist businesses strengthen their security defences, ethical hackers work within the confines of the law and moral principles.
  • Maintaining access: The goal of ethical hacking maintaining access phase is to prevent continued unauthorised access to the target system or network. This entails developing perseverance and keeping control after you've gained admission. They may learn more, make more thorough evaluations, and practise potential real-world assault scenarios by keeping access. In this stage, ethical hackers are able to find more vulnerabilities, assess the results of their activities, and make detailed recommendations on how to improve security measures and reduce risks.
  • Covering / clearing tracks:The goal of the ethical hacking phase known as "covering tracks" is to remove or alter any evidence of the activities involved in penetration testing. Ethical hackers take measures to get rid of any evidence that may point to their involvement in and use of the target system or network. The goal is to make sure that the customer or organisation may continue regular operations without any signs that an ethical hacking engagement ever took place. During this stage, ethical hackers follow the law and moral principles, refraining from any damaging or malevolent behaviour.
  • Reporting:A thorough report is created during the ethical hacking reporting phase to include the conclusions, processes, and suggestions. The report lists the vulnerabilities found, their seriousness, and any possible effects they may have on the target system or network. The report promotes communication, provides transparency, and directs the use of required security measures.

Skills for ethical hacking:

Understanding of web technologies, operating systems, programming languages (such Python or C/C++), networking, and VAPT tools like Nmap and Burp Suite are prerequisites for ethical hacking. It is essential to understand risk analysis, social engineering, and cryptography. To stay ahead of emerging dangers, continuous learning is required. Strong ethical principles and adherence to moral and legal restraints are essential for ethical hackers. Through self-study, real-world experience, and certifications like CEH, OSCP, or GPEN, skills can be built. Ethical hackers are essential in spotting weaknesses, boosting cybersecurity, and shielding organisations from dangerous actors since they have honed these capabilities.

Conclusion

In conclusion, ethical hacking is an essential technique that tries to find and fix weaknesses in computer networks, software, and systems. White hat hackers, sometimes referred to as ethical hackers, use their expertise to carry out approved and regulated penetration testing. They aid organisations in identifying flaws and possible security holes by simulating real- world attack scenarios. In order to proactively improve cybersecurity and stop unauthorised access, data breaches, and other cyber dangers, ethical hacking is essential. Ethical hackers help organisations strengthen their defences, safeguard sensitive data, and reduce risks by providing thorough reports and practical advice. By encouraging trust, resilience, and the protection of people, companies, and vital infrastructures, ethical hacking helps make the internet a safer place.
