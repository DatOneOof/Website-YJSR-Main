Chewing Gum: In Regards To The Human Brain

Posted by Rohini Vummadi on March 31, 2022

Introduction

Did you know that by chewing gum you can lose 11 calories per hour? Unfortunately, that’s not what I am here to talk about. I am here to discuss the brain in terms of chewing gum

Facts

1) Can increase blood flow to the brain, which is essential for the body to function properly.

2) Increases alertness.

3) Increase learning potential/capacity.

4) Helps with controlling mood.

5) An “anxiety buster.”

6) Reduces the feelings of depression.

7) Improves memory.

Case studies

1) A case study in 2009 showed that, under laboratory conditions, chewing gum resulted in decreased cortisol, the “stress hormone,” levels and a reduction in anxiety

2) A 2011 case study depicted by Live Science established that chewing gum prior to a test improves performance and concluded it was a form of warming up the brain. It does help prior to writing the test, but not throughout the test. If you continue to chew gum, exceeding the “warm-up” period, it would “nullify” the benefits.

3) “Researchers conducted a study where they used 224 undergraduates from St. Lawrence University to test the correlation between chewing gum and grades. They then divided these 224 subjects into three groups: “One chewed gum before and during the test, another chewed gum for five minutes before being tested and a third didn’t chew anything.” The subjects were then given a test to quantify their “brain power.” The researchers found that the students’ grades did improve, but only for a brief time. Researchers found that the gum “helped during recall and memory tasks especially.” Serge Onyper, who conducted the study at St. Lawrence University, stated that even though the chewing-gum group remembered more, in reality, they only remembered two-to-three more words. In the long run, two-to-three words can help you remember something so the number might seem small, but it does affect the score.”

General myths about chewing gum

1) If you swallow gum, it remains in your digestive tract for years.

2) Chewing gum causes cavities.

3) You can replace brushing with gum

Fun fact

Some say chewing gum can act like an antidepressant, but obviously without a prescription!
