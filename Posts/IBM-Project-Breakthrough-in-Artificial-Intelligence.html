IBM’s Project Debater: A Breakthrough in Artificial Intelligence and Natural Language Processing

Posted by Mridul Yadav on September 16, 2023

What is Project Debater?

Project Debater is an ambitious artificial intelligence project developed by IBM Research, with the goal of creating a system that can debate humans on complex topics. Project Debater aims to help people build persuasive arguments and make well-informed decisions by providing evidence-based reasoning and limiting the influence of emotion, bias, or ambiguity.

Project Debater was first proposed by Noam Slonim in 2011 as the next grand challenge for IBM Research, following the successes of Deep Blue and Watson. The project was officially launched in 2012, and involved a team of researchers from IBM’s lab in Haifa, Israel. Project Debater combines several natural language processing technologies, such as data-driven speech writing and delivery, listening comprehension, and modelling of human dilemmas, to perform the various tasks involved in a debate.

How does Project Debater work?

Project Debater relies on three pioneering capabilities: data-driven speech writing and delivery, listening comprehension, and the modelling of human dilemmas .

The first capability is data-driven speech writing and delivery, which enables the system to automatically generate a whole speech, reminiscent of an opinion article, and deliver it persuasively. The system uses machine learning techniques to mine massive corpora for claims and evidence, and uses them to construct arguments relevant to a given topic. The system also uses text-to-speech algorithms and techniques to give Project Debater a clear, fluent, and persuasive voice.

The second capability is listening comprehension, which allows the system to understand a long spontaneous speech made by the human opponent in order to construct a meaningful rebuttal. The system uses deep neural networks and weak supervision to train models that can identify the stance and sentiment of the opponent’s arguments, and use them to generate counter-arguments.

The third capability is the modelling of human dilemmas, which helps the system to form principled arguments based on a unique knowledge graph. The system uses natural language understanding and reasoning to extract dilemmas from a large collection of texts, and uses them to generate arguments that capture the essence of human debates.

By combining these core capabilities, Project Debater can conduct a meaningful debate with human debaters on various topics.

What are some of the achievements and applications of Project Debater?

Project Debater made its public debut in 2018, when it faced off against two human debaters, Noa Ovadia and Dan Zafrir, on the topics of government subsidies for space exploration and telemedicine. Project Debater was able to construct coherent speeches on both topics, using data from hundreds of millions of articles, and rebut some of the arguments made by its opponents. The audience voted that Project Debater enriched their knowledge more than the human debaters.

In 2019, Project Debater faced its ultimate challenge: debating Harish Natarajan, a world-class debater and former European debating champion, on the topic of preschool subsidies. The debate was held in front of a live audience at the Yerba Buena Centre for the Arts in San Francisco, and was broadcasted online to millions of viewers. Project Debater delivered a compelling case for preschool subsidies, citing studies and statistics to support its claims. However, Natarajan was able to counter Project Debater’s arguments with more specific examples and logical analysis. The audience voted that Natarajan was more persuasive than Project Debater, but also that Project Debater improved their knowledge more than Natarajan.

Project Debater is not only a remarkable achievement for artificial intelligence, but also a valuable tool for human decision making. Project Debater can help people explore different perspectives on controversial issues, synthesize large amounts of information, and generate concise and relevant summaries. Project Debater can also facilitate constructive dialogue and civil discourse among people with diverse opinions and backgrounds.

Some of the core technologies that power Project Debater are now available for commercial use through IBM Watson products and services. For example, key point analysis and narrative generation are offered as software services through the IBM Research Early Access Program. These technologies can help businesses and organizations analyse textual data from surveys, reviews, social media, and more, and generate insightful reports and recommendations.

What are some of the challenges and future directions of Project Debater?

Project Debater is an ongoing research project that continues to push the boundaries of artificial intelligence and natural language processing. The Project Debater team has published more than 30 peer-reviewed papers on various aspects of the project , including a cover article in Nature . The team also hosts online debates and experiments with the public to collect feedback and improve the system.

Some of the challenges that Project Debater faces include:

  • Handling noisy or incomplete data sources
  • Detecting sarcasm or irony in natural language
  • Generating creative or humorous arguments
  • Adapting to different languages or cultures
  • Evaluating the quality or impact of arguments

Some of the future directions that Project Debater aims to explore include:

  • Incorporating more ethical or moral reasoning
  • Supporting more interactive or collaborative modes of debate
  • Enhancing the emotional or social aspects of debate
  • Expanding the scope or diversity of topics
  • Integrating more multimodal or visual information

Project Debater is a testament to IBM’s vision of augmenting human intelligence with artificial intelligence, and creating a positive impact on society.
