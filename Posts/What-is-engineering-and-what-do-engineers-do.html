What is engineering and what do engineers do in each field?

Posted by Afreen Hossain on July 4, 2023

Let's explore the fascinating world of engineering!

Engineers are responsible for creating most of the products we use today, such as mobile phones, TVs, laptops, printers, and chargers. They possess problem-solving skills honed through mathematical training, which forms the foundation of engineering along with other subjects.

Engineers are equipped to solve problems by receiving a problem statement and devising solutions. This incredible feat is accomplished not by a single engineer but by a team of engineers working together.

Consider the invention of mobile phones as an example. A team of engineers recognized the challenge of connecting people around the world and enabling communication with loved ones, family members, and colleagues who may be on the other side of the globe. They perceived this as a problem and developed the solution of creating mobile phones.

Reginald Aubrey Fessenden, an Electrical Engineer and founder, achieved a groundbreaking milestone by creating the first wireless telephone device. He successfully transmitted a human voice over a distance of 1.6 km (one mile) using amplitude modulation, a technique that modifies the height of a wave to convey information, similar to adjusting the volume of a radio wave to transmit sound.

Now that you have gained an engineer's perspective, you have the ability to transform your ideas into reality. With further learning and upskilling, you can materialize whatever you desire. How amazing is that?

Let's delve into ten types of engineering, but keep in mind that this is not an exhaustive list:

  1. Aerospace Engineering: This branch of engineering focuses on designing, developing, and constructing aircraft, spacecraft, and related technologies. Aerospace engineers engage in tasks such as designing and testing aircraft structures, propulsion systems, control systems, aerodynamics, materials science, and avionics.

    • Designing and testing aircraft structures, such as wings and fuselage.
    • Developing propulsion systems, including engines and rocket motors.
    • Analyzing and simulating the performance and behavior of aerospace vehicles.
    • Designing control systems for aircraft and spacecraft.
    • Researching and developing new technologies for space exploration.

  2. Civil Engineering: Civil engineering involves designing, constructing, and maintaining infrastructure and public works projects. Civil engineers plan and oversee the construction of buildings, bridges, roads, dams, airports, water supply systems, and sewage systems, taking into account safety, functionality, sustainability, and economic feasibility.

    • Planning and designing buildings, bridges, and roads.
    • Overseeing the construction of infrastructure projects, such as dams and airports.
    • Evaluating the structural integrity and safety of buildings and bridges.
    • Developing sustainable and environmentally friendly construction practices.
    • Designing water supply and sewage systems for cities.

  3. Electrical Engineering: Electrical engineering encompasses the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. Electrical engineers work on areas such as power generation, transmission, and distribution systems, electronic circuits and devices, control systems, telecommunications, signal processing, and computer systems.

    • Designing and developing electronic circuits for various applications.
    • Creating power generation, transmission, and distribution systems.
    • Designing control systems for industrial processes and automation.
    • Developing telecommunications networks and systems.
    • Working on renewable energy technologies, such as solar panels and wind turbines.

  4. Marine Engineering: This branch of engineering focuses on the design, construction, operation, and maintenance of marine vessels and structures. Marine engineers are responsible for various aspects of marine technology, including propulsion systems, power generation, navigation and control systems, structural design, and safety measures.

    • Designing and constructing ships, submarines, and offshore platforms.
    • Developing propulsion systems and power generation systems for marine vessels.
    • Designing navigation and control systems for ships and submarines.
    • Ensuring the safety and stability of marine structures.
    • Conducting maintenance and repair work on marine equipment.

  5. Mechanical Engineering: Mechanical engineering involves the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems. Mechanical engineers work on machinery, energy systems, materials science, robotics, and automation. They consider factors such as efficiency, reliability, safety, and cost-effectiveness in their designs.

    • Designing and manufacturing machinery and mechanical systems.
    • Developing automotive components, such as engines and transmissions.
    • Conducting analysis and simulation to optimize the performance of mechanical systems.
    • Designing heating, ventilation, and air conditioning (HVAC) systems.
    • Working on robotics and automation technologies.

  6. Nanotechnology Engineering: Nanotechnology engineering specializes in manipulating matter at the nanoscale level. Nanotechnology engineers design, synthesize, characterize, and apply materials and devices at the nanoscale to create new functionalities and solve complex problems. They work with atomic and molecular structures to manipulate and control matter at extremely small scales.

    • Developing and manufacturing nanomaterials with specific properties.
    • Designing and fabricating nanoscale devices and sensors.
    • Researching and developing nanomedicine technologies.
    • Creating nanoelectronics and nanophotonics devices.
    • Investigating and applying nanotechnology in various industries, such as electronics, energy, and healthcare.

  7. Robotics Engineering: Robotics engineering focuses on the design, development, and operation of robots. Robotics engineers design the mechanical structure and components of robots, develop software and algorithms for controlling their behavior, and create intelligent machines capable of autonomous or guided tasks.

    • Designing and building robotic systems for industrial automation.
    • Developing autonomous robots for various applications, such as exploration and surveillance.
    • Creating robotic prosthetics and assistive devices for individuals with disabilities.
    • Programming and controlling robot behavior using algorithms and artificial intelligence.
    • Conducting research on human-robot interaction and collaboration.

  8. Software Engineering: Software engineering applies engineering principles and practices to the design, development, and maintenance of software systems. Software engineers analyze user requirements, design software architectures, write code, perform testing and debugging, and deploy and maintain software systems.

    • Developing software applications and systems for different platforms.
    • Designing software architectures and database structures.
    • Writing code and programming languages to implement software functionalities.
    • Testing and debugging software for quality assurance.
    • Maintaining and updating software systems to meet user needs and security requirements.

  9. Biomedical Engineering: Biomedical engineering combines engineering principles with medical sciences to design and improve medical devices, diagnostic tools, therapeutic solutions, and healthcare systems. Biomedical engineers work on applications such as medical imaging technologies, prosthetics, artificial organs, drug delivery systems, and rehabilitation devices.

    • Designing and developing medical imaging equipment, such as MRI and CT scanners.
    • Creating prosthetics and artificial limbs for improved mobility.
    • Developing medical devices for monitoring and diagnosing health conditions.
    • Designing drug delivery systems for precise and targeted treatment.
    • Conducting research on tissue engineering and regenerative medicine.

  10. Chemical Engineering: Chemical engineering integrates chemistry, physics, biology, and mathematics to design and optimize processes for the production, transformation, and utilization of chemicals, materials, and energy. Chemical engineers work in various industries, including pharmaceuticals, food and beverages, energy production, environmental engineering, and manufacturing, creating efficient and sustainable processes.

    • Designing chemical processes for manufacturing various products, such as pharmaceuticals and plastics.
    • Developing sustainable and environmentally friendly manufacturing methods.
    • Designing and optimizing chemical reactors and separation systems.
    • Conducting research on new materials and catalysts.
    • Working on energy production and storage technologies.

Remember, these ten types of engineering are just a glimpse into the diverse and expansive field of engineering. There are many more branches and areas to explore if this introduction to engineering has piqued your interest in how engineers contribute to the world.
