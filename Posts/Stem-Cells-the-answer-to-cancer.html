Stem cells: The Answer To Cancer?

The Answer to Cancer We’ve Been Waiting For? A Deep Dive into the Latest Breakthroughs

Posted by Tamera Simon on July 23, 2023

Introduction

In the human body, cells divide and multiply as necessary throughout our lives. Normally, cells perish when they become abnormal or aged. However, there are instances when certain cells continue to produce new cells while the old or irregular cells fail to undergo the expected cell death. This uncontrolled cell division is responsible for the formation of cancerous tumors. Dating back to 3000 BC, cancer has long been recognized as one of the most deadly diseases. This is because cancer cells can outgrow and displace normal cells when their growth becomes uncontrollable. However, recent studies in scientific research have shed light on the potential of stem cells in improving cancer treatment.

What are Stem Cells?

Stem cells are ‘empty’ cells that are capable of generating specialized cells with unique functions. In both natural physiological processes and controlled laboratory environments, stem cells possess the remarkable ability to divide and give rise to ‘daughter’ cells.

These daughter cells can either develop into new stem cells or transform into specialized cells that fulfill specific roles within the body. This allows for the formation of a variety of cell types, such as blood cells, brain cells, heart muscle cells, and bone cells. This allows stem cells to continue in the repair or even create new tissue and organs.

3 types of stem cells work in the body. They are autologous, Allogeneic, and syngeneic. Autologous cells come directly from the patient's body, allogeneic come from a donor, and syngeneic would be from a patient's identical twin if they had one.

How can stem cells help cancer

Stem cell transplantation is a promising approach that indirectly aids cancer patients in their battle against the disease. By infusing healthy cells into the patient's bloodstream, stem cell transplantation helps to replace the damaged cells in the body, thereby boosting the patient's immune system and enabling them to receive chemotherapy.

However, the success of this treatment depends largely on the type of stem cells being used. Different types of stem cells possess unique capabilities in terms of cellular differentiation and regeneration. As such, it is essential to carefully consider the most suitable type of stem cell for transplantation, as this decision can greatly impact the overall outcome of the treatment.

Moreover, stem cell transplantation is a complex process that requires significant expertise and resources. It involves collecting stem cells from a donor and then processing them to ensure their purity and safety before they can be administered to the patient. The patient's immune system must also be suppressed to prevent rejection of the transplanted cells, which can cause complications.

Despite these challenges, stem cell transplantation holds great promise in improving the treatment outcomes for cancer patients. Ongoing research is focused on developing new and improved stem cell therapies that can be customized to the specific needs of individual patients.

CSCs

Cancerous stem cells can form after treatment which can cause patients who are in remission to relapse back. This is because these cells continue to multiply and never stop. This continued multiplication allows it to affect the body more quickly. CSCs are also very resilient against treatments such as chemotherapy and radiation. This resistance contributes to their ability to survive and initiate tumor regrowth, ultimately leading to disease recurrence. Because of the existence of CSCs researchers are actively exploring novel approaches, such as targeted therapies and combination treatments, to effectively tackle CSCs and prevent relapse in cancer patients.

Conclusion

Although stem cell transplant would be helpful for many different cancers, so far stem cell transplantation has only shown promise in treating leukemia, lymphoma, multiple myeloma, and neuroblastoma. Researchers are exploring applications in other cancer types and conducting clinical trials to assess their efficacy and expand treatment options. By investigating the use of stem cell transplantation in different cancer types, scientists strive to expand treatment options and improve outcomes for patients across the spectrum of cancer diseases.
