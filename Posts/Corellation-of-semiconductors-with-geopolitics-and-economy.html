Abstract

Semiconductors have driven the growth and development of the entire world. They are substances which have the perfect magnitude of electrical conductivity to create microchips used in electronic devices. Now that we live in the digital age, we are surrounded by semiconductors, whether it is in the form of smartphones, laptops or cars. Such an integral part of human life is bound to have implications on global policy and economy. Especially because, like every other valuable commodity, it is limited and the world has recently been battling a chip shortage. The industry of semiconductors operates through a global value chain network, wherein multiple regions contribute in the development, manufacturing and distribution of chips. Semiconductors have made the world more interconnected and economically stronger. On the other hand, their scarcity has aggravated inﬂation and proved disastrous to manufacturing industries. Nations try to gain control of the semiconductor industry for their advantage, which poses a threat to international security. This has been observed in the tussle between China and Taiwan, where China is trying to get uniﬁed with the neighbouring country, signiﬁcantly because it is the world's largest semiconductor manufacturer. If a war were to break out between these two nations, it would have grave repercussions for the entire world. Therefore, it is imperative to study how the semiconductor industry impacts the world because of its pertinence over each of our lives. This article delves into these impacts and determines how the semiconductor industry is interrelated to geographical politics as well as the global economy.

Introduction

Semiconductors are a revolution in technological advancement and a pillar for both geopolitical power and economic aﬄuence. They are the building blocks of all electronic devices. Used in the form of microchips, semiconductors are those substances that conduct electricity under special circumstances, like an adequate amount of heat or a magnetic ﬁeld. Their conductivity lies between a conductor and an insulator, making them perfect for managing and controlling electric current in electronic devices. Some examples of semiconductors are silicon, germanium, carbon, arsenic, etc.

These nano-sized silicon chips are powerful components that serve as the backbone of our modern technological ﬁelds, enabling the functioning of countless electronic devices and driving innovation across industries like Information & Technology, health, ﬁnance, automobiles, and defence. Whether it is smartphones, computers, televisions, cars, medical devices, military equipment, or an array of other essential technologies that shape our daily lives, semiconductors are omnipresent. As the global demand for advanced electronics continues to surge, the network of semiconductor manufacturing and distribution, also known as the supply chain, has been expanding.

The current geopolitical scene is permeated by volatility, conﬂict, and uncertainty. The Russia-Ukraine war has further strained a multitude of international relationships. Since all industries across the world use electronic devices that use semiconductors, microchips navigate throughout the globe. The Nation that establishes dominion over chips shall end up having an inordinate strategic advantage over other countries, which it can use for the exploitation of others and personal gain. Thus, the semiconductor industry has a critical role in this scenario. The world is facing a distressing semiconductor shortage which, if not dealt with sensitively, may propel the world to the brink of war.

This also poses a threat to the world economy, which is facing a slowdown. A recession is looming, and the world is still dealing with the remnants of the COVID-19 pandemic. In light of the current situation, the rising demand and the simultaneous shortage of microchips can prove detrimental to the global economy. Due to the dependency of a massive number of industries on the semiconductor industry, it is pivotal to the global economy and world trade. Even though semiconductors by themselves constitute a small portion of world trade and GDP, they form an imperative component of commodities and processes worth trillions of dollars. Thus, disruptions in the semiconductor industry can seep into other sectors and exacerbate the situation by reducing productivity and increasing unemployment. It is, therefore, important that the semiconductor industry stays sturdy for a steady economic ecosystem.

Semiconductors have a vital role in ensuring the economic and geopolitical stability of the world. Therefore, this shortage should be dealt with innovatively and sustainably. To achieve this, the products need to be redesigned, research and development need to be expedited, and the supply chain needs to become streamlined and balanced. It is vital for everyone to be aware of the relevance of semiconductors to the world, the economy, and all of our lives. Semiconductors may be a threat, but it is an even greater opportunity to shape the world in the right manner and ensure the welfare as well as the progress of the entire world. Thus, it is rightly said that semiconductors are the oil of the new world.

Geopolitical Impact

Since the latter half of the 20th century, semiconductors have acted as a link between all nations. Every country needs microchips to drive its technological growth and development. Thus, it plays a decisive role in the sculpting of foreign policies and international relations. The fact that only a few nations hold a monopoly over the production of quality chips gives rise to a plethora of geopolitical implications. Any geographical shift in technological manufacturing leads directly to a shift in power dynamics. This is the reason why Europe dominated the world in the 19th century, America in the 20th, and now Asia is emerging as the new nucleus of power. It can, thus, be construed that semiconductors have impacted geopolitics in more ways than one.

Interconnected World and Global Interaction

Semiconductors have accelerated globalisation by increasing interconnectedness and interdependence among countries and economies. Microchips have enhanced worldwide communication, transportation and trade links through the advancement of technology. Apart from this, they interlinked the world as a consequence of their diversiﬁed supply chains. The raw material for chips comes from a particular region, the manufacturing takes place in another region and they are shipped to a diﬀerent region, on the basis of specialisation and demand. Cognizant of this, semiconductor-producing companies have been crossing national borders for each supply chain activity. Not only has this paved the way for a more globalised world, but it has also boosted global trade as a result of better access and dispersion of microchips, which contribute to the development of other commodities. The growth charts of the Sales Revenue of semiconductors (Fig.1)1 and of the Total Global Exports (Fig.2)2 are in congruence with each other. It can, therefore, be construed that with the advent of semiconductors, the world has become more interconnected and interdependent.

Global Chip Shortage and its Implications

The recent developments in the post-COVID world ensued an acute shortage of semiconductors. Millions of workers became homebound, impeding the production of microchips. Moreover, the Russia-Ukraine war and the Taiwan droughts of 2021 disturbed the supply chain. On the other hand, the demand for electronic devices surged in an unprecedented manner due to a multitude of reasons like work-from-home and the requirement of data-mining machines for cryptocurrency. Personal computers alone saw a 13% hike in global demand. This situation came to be known as the Global Semiconductor Crisis or ‘Chipageddon’.

Such a situation can have serious consequences on geopolitical harmony. It will escalate geopolitical competition among countries for the control of the semiconductor industry so that they can shield themselves from the crisis and leverage the shortage by monopolising the industry and exports in order to exert supremacy. A good example of this is pressurisation on the Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) by the United States to stop supplying chips to the Chinese company Huawei, due to the tensions between the two nations.

The Value Chain and its Signiﬁcance

A value chain or a supply chain refers to the intricate network of all the processes and stages involved in the production and distribution of semiconductor devices and chips. The world has become an interconnected economy, and the value chain has had a signiﬁcant role in it. The value chain of semiconductors is intricate, interconnected as well as dispersed globally. It entails research and development, design and manufacturing of microchips. Diﬀerent nations specialise in diﬀerent areas. Countries like India, Israel, Singapore, Germany, South Korea, and the UK have become the vanguards of semiconductor R&D. Canada, European nations, and the United States lead the world in design. At the forefront of manufacturing, assembling, testing, and supply of raw materials are countries like Taiwan, China, Malaysia, Japan, and other Asian nations. It is, therefore, vital for all countries to collaborate to utilise the expertise of every geographical region for the beneﬁt of the entire Earth. The synergy between the diﬀerent global semiconductor industries is massive. In fact, it is postulated that the semiconductor crisis aggravated as an outcome of the ripple eﬀect generated by the March 2021 ﬁre at a Tokyo factory of Renesas Electronics, a company that controls 35% of the automotive semiconductor market.

Chip Wars: Why are Chips a cause of Conﬂict between Nations?

Japan imposed export restrictions on South Korea in 2019 due to a diplomatic conﬂict. Since South Korean companies sorely needed raw materials from Japan for chip production. Thus, Japan had to ease the restrictions in 2020. However, semiconductors do not always resolve conﬂicts. As mentioned earlier, countries like to assert dominance over others and breach the value chain to emerge self-suﬃcient. This leads to a situation known as chip wars, wherein nations formulate policies, impose sanctions and take actions that undermine the position of other nations in the global value chain. The ongoing US-China Conﬂict is the quintessential example of a chip war. The United States has imposed trade restrictions on Chinese companies. In retaliation, China has expelled foreign vendors from its domestic markets and given exclusivity to Chinese ﬁrms in the regulation of manufacturing. India is also planning to combat and emerge as an alternative to China by announcing a $10 billion incentive plan to amplify semiconductor manufacturing.

Boon and Bane for National Security

National security in today’s day and age is a very broad concept; it encompasses human security, military security, economic security, environment security, food security and information security. Semiconductors have been helpful in all these domains because of their usage in missile systems, surveillance, secure communication, modernisation of agriculture and boosting items of export. Due to the interconnected technological advancements brought about by semiconductors in all domains of national security, nations have experienced prosperity, well-being, and improvements in overall health and welfare.

There is, however, a drawback to interconnected economies, value chains can be tampered with, and the supply of semiconductors can be distorted by any nation with undue dominance. This will not only disrupt other industrial processes but also deprive nations of exploiting vital know-hows, which are diﬃcult to import—for example, quality chip production, R&D, management techniques, et cetera. Thus, there might arise a global crisis, which can potentially lead to war-like situations. Apart from this, counterfeit versions of chips may exist and carry viruses or malware that can undermine the security of a nation. Another example of a threat is the integration of semiconductor technology in mechanised and AI-based warfare, which can be more lethal than the contemporary practices of warfare. Therefore, the impact of semiconductors on the security of a nation is lopsided.

Economic Impact

Economic Growth

Semiconductors are estimated to have a trillion-dollar market by 2030, currently standing at $580.13 billion in 2022. Semiconductors do not constitute a large portion of the global GDP; however, it has doubled from 0.25% of the GDP three decades ago to 0.50% in 2021. The role of semiconductors can be understood with the examples of countries like Taiwan and the US. Taiwan, also often referred to as the "Silicon Valley of Asia," has the most dominant presence in the global semiconductor market. The semiconductor industry has played a humongous role in Taiwan’s economy and growth. A renowned Taiwanese company, Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC), has become a big player in semiconductor manufacturing, supplying chips to major technology companies globally. Taiwan's strong semiconductor industry has also attracted foreign investment providing it with building a better supply chain and strengthening its economy. Similarly, in the United States, the semiconductor industry has played a key role in economic growth and innovation. It has been a signiﬁcant contributor to the GDP and employment. Additionally, the export of semiconductors has contributed to a positive trade balance for the United States, supporting its position as a global technology leader.

Shortage of Semiconductors as a Cause of Inﬂation

The emergence of the semiconductor industry has had an intense economic impact, particularly with regard to the inﬂationary eﬀects on the automobile and electronics sectors. About 25% of manufacturing industries depend on semiconductors directly for their inputs. The surge in demand and the global semiconductor shortage caused a supply-demand imbalance and triggered inﬂationary pressures within these industries. The industries heavily reliant on semiconductors have faced inﬂation at a higher rate, as indicated by Fig.33. Moreover, the electronics sector has also been severely impacted, with prices of electronic devices, including smartphones and laptops, experiencing an upward trajectory due to the shortage of semiconductors. This inﬂationary trend has resulted in reduced aﬀordability for consumers and hindered the growth potential of the electronics industry.

Automobile Industry

The automobile industry was one of the most aﬀected industries by the semiconductor crisis. The shortage of semiconductors rattled the production of automobile manufacturers, leading to reduced manufacturing and increased prices of vehicles, thereby aﬀecting consumer purchasing power and bounding the growth of the automobile industry. The prices of cars rose about 20% worldwide annually, since the commencement of the shortage. Car manufacturers have had to cut an estimated 19.6 million vehicles out of their production schedules between 2021 and 2023. In 2021, the automotive industry lost approximately $200 billion of revenue due to the aforementioned crisis. In the same year, India recorded the lowest levels of automobile sales in the proceeding ten years.

China-Taiwan Conﬂict: Case-study Analysis

Overview of Cross-Strait Relations

The diplomatic relations between China and Taiwan are known as the Cross-Strait Relations. Taiwan is an island nation located in the South China Sea and it has an independent governance of its own. However, the People's Republic of China (PRC) claims that it is a part of the PRC because of its historic and ethnic ties. A central role in China's calls for a "national reuniﬁcation" is the fact that Taiwan is the largest manufacturer of semiconductors. It produces 60 per cent of the total semiconductors and 90 per cent of the most advanced ones. Thus, dominion over Taiwan will give China a colossal strategic advantage. Both India and the USA do not oﬃcially recognise Taiwan, however, most nations maintain informal ties because of its strategic importance. According to Taiwan's President Tsai Ing-wen, the Taiwanese chip industry is a "silicon shield" that protects it from "authoritative regimes" trying to sabotage the supply chain.

In case of a war or blockade

Taiwan is a vital link in the global value chain and any threat to its sovereignty, in the words of UK’s foreign secretary James Cleverly, shall mean that “no country could shield itself from the repercussions of a war in Taiwan”. Invasion of the island nation will be catastrophic to its successful democracy and the human rights of its people. The Taiwanese semiconductor manufacturing industry having a net worth of $521.93B shall become endangered. About 50% of the world’s container ships pass through the Taiwan Strait. This implies that a war or blockade on this strait would cripple $2.6 trillion worth of world trade. Therefore, this conﬂict will elicit interventions from all of the major economies of the world. Such a situation can have more grave repercussions on the world economy and will set the economy back 20 years.

Conclusion

Semiconductor microchips changed the world as we knew it. It is the catalyst that has driven the overall technological advancement of the world. From defence and agriculture to automobiles and smartphones, microchips are used everywhere and touch our lives every day. The entire world collaborates to leverage every region’s strength to produce and distribute quality semiconductor chips, creating an intricate value chain. This has made our world more interconnected and globalised. As a consequence of this, the geopolitical environment is also aﬀected. Countries interact with each other in a manner that grants them some inﬂuence over the global value chain. The Semiconductor Crisis of 2021 also has signiﬁcant geopolitical ramiﬁcations like international conﬂict and threats to the security of nations. Apart from geopolitics, semiconductors have also had huge impacts on the global economy, be it improved trade or inﬂation and production deﬁcit as a consequence of chip shortage. Semiconductors also play an integral role in international disputes. The chip industry of Taiwan is a determinant in China’s insistence on their uniﬁcation. This may lead to a tussle on the Taiwanese Strait, that can disturb the semiconductor industry and give rise to grave repercussions to the world. Thus, semiconductors have a pivotal role in the tale of our century. The impact of microchips is massive, cohesive and pervasive. It has the capability of stirring up havoc in our world as well as opening avenues for a new age of intelligence, what matters is how the human race decides to use it.
