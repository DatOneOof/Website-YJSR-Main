TECHNOLOGY AND COMMUNICATION IN TODAYâ€™S WORLD

Posted by Ritika Tyagi on August 6, 2023

Information:

In the modern world, technology has made communication simpler, but at the sacrifice of face-to-face interaction. As this form of communication enables them to handle their own affairs, remain at home, and communicate with others who are hundreds of miles away, more and more people are choosing to engage from home in front of a computer screen. However, a lot of people start utilising technology for communication before they really need to. In actuality, people forgo face-to-face engagement in favour of using technology for communications. Information technologies and various forms of distant communication have made it possible to communicate with people who are far away, but many people abuse this type of communication and forgo crucial personal interactions.

Technologies facilitate communication:-

Technology and culture are two particular factors that affect how people communicate. Interpersonal communication, small-group communication, organisational communication, mass communication, and public communication are the six basic categories of communication where innovative technologies are used. These types can work together and none of them exist alone.

Prior until a few years ago, it is clear that each of these forms of communication had its limitations due to personal contact, telephone discussions when a person's mobility was restricted, and written correspondence. Nowadays, people may use the Internet to speak information, making communication at each of these levels easier. For instance, a corporation shouldn't call everyone together to deliver a single, straightforward announcement.

All workers' emails only need to be sent via corporate email. Prior to the ability to deliver messages via information technology, public and organisational borders in communication were the most difficult to navigate. Managers of organisations are not required to limit their work and tell their subordinates. It is possible to set up a video conference or send a message so that everyone in the meeting can voice their opinions. It is simple since people can go more quickly. Additionally, all the necessary tools are still available.

Technologies create complication in communication :-

The internet, together with a number of supplementary programmes and software, has improved and built a good foundation for remote communication. Despite being based on an overly complicated programme and domain, the Internet has in no way made communication simpler. People may communicate with each other regardless of where they are thanks to tools like ICQ, Skype, Facebook, and other similar services.

The communications are now simpler, quicker, and more practical thanks to all of these services. Through online communication, people have the chance to consider their responses before acting. Additionally, a choice might be made later on after consulting something or someone online. Therefore, it is possible to assert that online communication enables people to make well-considered decisions.

Due to certain factors, online communication is also an excellent way to stay in touch with those who are far away. In addition, because of the technology's ability to send both speech and video, it is also feasible to view friends and family members. Isn't it wonderful to have a sense of proximity to those who live thousands of kilometres away?

Since people are social beings, they require personal contact in order to feel other people's touch, observe other people's gestures, etc. Written communication cannot convey intonation or convey how people are responding to what is being spoken. Communication becomes more challenging as a result; occasionally, people are unable to comprehend one another and must repeat themselves, which takes too much time.

Conclusion:-

It follows that even if new technology have made communication more convenient in terms of place and time, people still experience problems with it. Online communication lacks the intonation and mimicry that are crucial in real-world talks. People don't actually communicate in ways they have to these days, despite the paradox that it has gotten easier and faster. They appear to communicate facts without emotion. Smiles and other graphic images are used to replace the reaction, which does not accurately represent the true feelings. Additionally, since they are not required to express their response, they may not feel any feelings.
