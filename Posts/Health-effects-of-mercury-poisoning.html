Health Effects of Mercury Poisoning

Posted by Min Khant Oakkar on May 27, 2022

Introduction to Mercury

Mercury in 3 forms

Mercury can be found in three different forms in the environment which vary in effects on the human body and characteristics.

Elemental

Elemental mercury is the kind that has not formed with anything and is still in its initial element form: liquid at room temperature/pressure, heavy, metallic substance. Generally, a small amount of metallic mercury may cause some neurological diseases; however, a larger amount may damage the major organs.

Inorganic

Inorganic mercury can be found primarily in the skincare product industries as mercuric salts of mercury combined with sulfur or oxygen. These substances are ionized (positively charged) which is more harmful to gastrointestinal parts.

Organic

Organic mercury, also known as methylmercury, is extremely toxic and can be found in the environment. A tiny drop of it can cause severe health problems that can lead to death.

The root of mercury poisoning

Mercury poisoning results from exposure to the element, either directly or through the environment.

  • Broken thermometers
    • Exposure to the liquid inside glass thermometers
  • Artisanal mining
    • Contact with the chemical in the form of vapor
  • Skincare products
    • Certain types of skin-lightening creams and anti-aging products contain mercury in them
  • Toxic air in specific communities
    • Factories and other work environments where the employees are exposed to mercury in the atmosphere
  • Modern wares
    • Batteries, fluorescent light bulbs
  • Mercury-containing fish
    • Fish consume mercury from the water they live which is contaminated by the aforementioned factories and environments
    • The bigger the fish, the higher concentration of mercury in it since it preys on other mercury-containing fish

Development of the poison in the human body

Risk Factors

Depending on the type of person exposed to it or the type of mercury they are exposed to, mercury can have different effects. The higher risk of mercury poisoning shows in:

  • Unborn babies and young children:
    Children are more likely to get mercury poisoning than adults due to being in the stage of development in the nervous system which is the main target of the effect.
  • Workers in mercury-exposing environments:
    Even if a person is not directly in contact with mercury, the vapor can cause extreme effects and severe diseases.
  • People with kidney diseases.

Symptoms

  • Mental instability: depression, mood swings, anxiety
  • Memory loss
  • Numbness
  • Hearing difficulties
  • Muscle weakness
  • Vision changes

Mercury poisoning mainly leads to intense neurological damage, such as low IQ, damaged motor skills, paralysis, amnesia, and lagging reflexes. Additionally, the chemical can also affect the cardiovascular and reproductive systems, reduce sperm count, cause danger to the fetus, and lessen its survival rate.

Conclusion

Mercury can have damaging effects on our bodies, which is why you might want to consume less seafood. Since mercury can be detrimental to the reproductive system, pregnant women should try to avoid mercury-containing fish in their meals. For further prevention, blood and urine tests should be carried out. Moreover, it is suggested to reduce the amount of mercury exposure whether at home or at work for the sake of one’s health.

Fun fact: Mercury poisoning was formally known as “Mad hatter disease,” an extreme case of erethism, during the 18th century due to its harsh effects on hat makers whose work processes include the usage of mercury.
