The Metaverse: Can Technology Save An Island

Digital Dimensions and Island Preservation: Unleashing the Potential of the Metaverse

Posted by Ariana Walcott on June 6, 2023

Tuvalu is faced with one unstoppable problem: Climate Change.

Tuvalu, the fourth smallest country that is located in Oceania, is in danger. Right now, It is isolated and surrounded by the sea. Soon, it will be gone because of rising sea levels. Sea levels, as of now, do not have the capabilities of overtaking major cities such as New York Or Washington DC. Though scientists predict Tuvalu could become uninhabitable in the next 50 to 100 years, locals say they feel it could be more sooner. [1]

To fight this situation, preserve the country’s culture, and land, locals are forced to turn to technology: the Metaverse. It is a simulated digital environment that combines augmented reality (AR), virtual reality (VR), blockchain, and social media principles to create areas for rich user interaction that imitate the real world. [2] In a widely popular video, Simon Kofe, Tuvalu’s foreign minister explains the plan to digitize the country. [3] Kofe further explains this decision to provide solace to the Tuvalu people and remind them of the country’s once-rich culture. He urgently requests a global effort to make sure the Tuvalu people aren’t forced to do this.

But sea levels aren’t the only consequence of Climate Change. Because of sea levels, crops and coastal homes are constantly in risk of being destroyed. The damaging effects of climate cause an inability to plant crops, slowly making the country uninhabitable. Soon the country will become the first digital nation and not by choice. And so, hundreds of the Tuvaluan people will be forced to evacuate their homes and seek refuge.

How to Help Tuvalu

Besides donating, there are ways to help Tuvalu. Here’s how:

  1. Utilize social media. Social media is one of the strongest weapons when it comes to social justice and climate justice. Even just making a post can help with rallying a cause to save Tuvalu.
  2. Reposting. Reposting content spreads the message farther and can make others feel more inclined to the same.
  3. Hosting a protest. Holding a peaceful protest not only builds supporters but brings awareness.
  4. Reaching out to your district and/or state officials. Finding and contacting your state officials can go a long way. See what they support and ask for help.
  5. Last but not least, stay updated on climate news and specifically on any United Nation Climate Change Conference.
